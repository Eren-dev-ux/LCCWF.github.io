Our Programmatic Approach

LCCWF designs and implements evidence-informed programs targeting critical social challenges affecting children and families in marginalized settings. Our work is structured around four interconnected pillars:

Child Protection

Safeguarding children's rights and wellbeing

Community Health

Promoting wellness and preventive care

Education & Youth

Empowering through knowledge and skills

Community Empowerment

Building sustainable livelihoods

Program Area 1

Child Protection & Development

Protecting vulnerable children and promoting their holistic development

Key Objectives

  • Advocate for and protect children's rights
  • Provide psychosocial support to vulnerable children
  • Develop life skills and resilience in children
  • Support orphans and children with special needs
  • Prevent and respond to child abuse and neglect

Program Components

Child Rights Advocacy

Community awareness campaigns on children's rights and protection laws.

Psychosocial Support

Counseling services and support groups for children affected by trauma.

Life Skills Education

Training in decision-making, communication, and problem-solving skills.

Mentorship Programs

Positive role models and guidance for at-risk children and youth.

Aligns with:

SDG 3 SDG 4 SDG 10
Program Area 2

Community Health & Wellness

Promoting physical, mental, and social wellbeing in underserved communities

Key Objectives

  • Reduce substance use and related harms
  • Improve maternal and child health outcomes
  • Promote mental health and psychosocial wellbeing
  • Enhance community hygiene and sanitation
  • Increase access to preventive healthcare

Program Components

Substance Use Prevention

Evidence-based prevention programs for youth, parents, and communities.

Maternal & Child Health

Prenatal care, nutrition support, and child immunization services.

Mental Health Services

Counseling, support groups, and community mental health awareness.

Health Education

Workshops on disease prevention, nutrition, and healthy lifestyles.

Aligns with:

SDG 3 SDG 5 SDG 10

Substance Use Prevention Focus

Our flagship program targets children, adolescents, parents, and caregivers with evidence-informed prevention strategies, behavioral change communication, and community awareness campaigns to reduce substance-related harms.

Program Area 3

Education & Youth Empowerment

Creating opportunities for learning, leadership, and lifelong success

Key Objectives

  • Increase access to quality education
  • Reduce school dropout rates
  • Develop youth leadership skills
  • Provide vocational and life skills training
  • Support educational continuity for vulnerable children

Program Components

Educational Support

Scholarships, learning materials, and tutoring for at-risk students.

School Retention

Programs to prevent dropout and support reintegration of out-of-school children.

Youth Leadership

Training in leadership, public speaking, and community engagement.

Skills Development

Vocational training in market-relevant skills for economic empowerment.

Aligns with:

SDG 4 SDG 5 SDG 10
Program Area 4

Community Empowerment & Livelihoods

Building self-reliance and sustainable community development

Key Objectives

  • Strengthen community capacity and ownership
  • Create sustainable livelihood opportunities
  • Empower women and caregivers economically
  • Build resilient community systems
  • Promote inclusive community development

Program Components

Skills Training

Income-generating skills training in agriculture, crafts, and small business.

Women Empowerment

Programs focused on economic independence and leadership for women.

Community Mobilization

Capacity building for community leaders and local organizations.

Social Enterprise

Support for community-based enterprises and cooperatives.

Aligns with:

SDG 5 SDG 10 SDG 17
